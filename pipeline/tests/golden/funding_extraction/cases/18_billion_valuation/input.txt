Skyvault raises $95M as demand for sovereign cloud storage grows

Skyvault, the encrypted-storage company, said this week it has raised $95 million in
new funding led by Halcyon Growth, valuing the company at $1.2 billion. Existing
investor Bay Laurel Capital also participated.

The company sells storage that lets customers keep encryption keys entirely on their
own hardware, a pitch that has resonated with European governments and healthcare
systems.

Skyvault said annual recurring revenue has doubled in the past year, driven by
regulated industries moving off shared infrastructure.
