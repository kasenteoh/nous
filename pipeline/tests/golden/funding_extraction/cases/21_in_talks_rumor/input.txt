Meridian Labs, the agent-infrastructure startup behind the Hermes developer
platform, is in talks to raise new funding at a valuation of about $1.5
billion, according to three people familiar with the discussions.

The company is in discussions with existing and new investors to raise
roughly $75 million, the people said, cautioning that the round has not yet
closed and terms could still change. Meridian Labs declined to comment.

Founded in 2023, Meridian Labs has raised $25 million to date from seed
investors, and its Hermes platform is used by several Fortune 500 pilot
programs. The prospective round would nearly triple the valuation the
company commanded last year.
