Grocery-delivery startup Fernwheel is shutting down

Fernwheel, which promised 15-minute grocery delivery in four cities, told employees on
Friday that it is shutting down operations at the end of the month after failing to
secure new financing. The company had raised $25 million to date from investors
including Cannonball Ventures and Left Coast Capital.

"We could not make the unit economics work in a capital-constrained market," CEO Dana
Ferro wrote in a note to staff. Roughly 180 employees will be affected. Fernwheel said
it is working to place workers with delivery and logistics companies in its markets.
