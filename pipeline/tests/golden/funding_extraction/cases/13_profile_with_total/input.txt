How Ostrander's founder turned a spreadsheet habit into an insurance platform

When Priya Ostrander started automating her agency's renewals in a spreadsheet, she
didn't plan to build a company. Eight years later, Ostrander is one of the
better-known names in insurance software, and its founder still reviews customer
tickets every Friday.

The company has raised $120 million to date from investors including Accel and
Founders Fund, and now employs about 350 people across three offices. Revenue, the
company says, has grown every quarter since launch.

Asked about an IPO, Ostrander demurs: "We like building. The rest will take care of
itself."
