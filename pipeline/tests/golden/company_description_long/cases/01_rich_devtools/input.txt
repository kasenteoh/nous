Pipewright — See inside every build

Pipewright is a CI/CD observability platform for engineering teams that run
their builds on GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, or Jenkins. It ingests every
pipeline event and turns them into a searchable timeline: which jobs ran,
what they cost, which tests flaked, and where the queue backed up.

Why teams pick Pipewright

Modern teams merge dozens of times a day, and a slow or flaky pipeline taxes
every merge. Most teams debug their CI by scrolling raw logs. Pipewright
replaces that with structured build telemetry: each step is traced, tagged,
and stored, so an engineer can ask "which test slowed down this week" and get
an answer in seconds.

How it works

A lightweight collector runs as a step in your existing pipeline
configuration — no agent daemons, no proxy. It streams timing, cache hits,
artifact sizes, and test results to Pipewright's backend, where a columnar
store keeps 13 months of history. Flake detection replays failing tests in
isolation on ephemeral runners and marks the nondeterministic ones
automatically. Dashboards break down duration by repository, branch, and
team, and alert rules fire on regressions against a rolling baseline.

Who uses it

Platform engineering teams at companies between 20 and 2,000 engineers use
Pipewright to cut CI spend and keep merge queues moving. Documented use cases
run from monorepo build sharding to nightly-release verification.

Pricing

Free for open-source projects and up to 3 developers. The Team plan is $30
per developer per month; Enterprise adds SSO, audit logs, support for
self-hosted runners, and a dedicated Slack channel.

About

Pipewright was founded in 2022 in Seattle, WA by two ex-infrastructure
engineers who ran build systems at a large e-commerce company and grew tired
of debugging CI through log archaeology. The team is 14 people, remote-first.

Compared to rolling your own

Teams sometimes assemble similar visibility from Grafana dashboards and
homegrown scripts. Pipewright's pitch is that build telemetry is a product,
not a side project: the schema, the flake detector, and the cost attribution
come ready, and the collector installs in under ten minutes.
