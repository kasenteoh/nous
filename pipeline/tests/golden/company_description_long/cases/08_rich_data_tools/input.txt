Rowfern — Ship data pipelines you can trust

Rowfern is testing and contract enforcement for data pipelines. It catches
broken schemas, silent nulls, and volume anomalies before they reach your
dashboards — in the pull request, not the postmortem.

What Rowfern does

Rowfern reads your dbt project or SQL pipelines and generates a suite of
checks: schema contracts between producing and consuming models, freshness
and volume expectations learned from history, and assertions you write in
YAML. Checks run in CI on every pull request against a sampled snapshot of
production data, so a column rename or a join that fans out gets caught
before merge. In production, the same checks run on schedule and page the
owning team through PagerDuty or Slack when a contract breaks.

Open core

The check engine is open source under an Apache 2.0 license. The commercial
platform adds the catalog of contracts, ownership routing, historical
baselines, and SSO.

Who uses it

Data platform teams at Chartbeam, Densify Analytics, and Copperline Health
run Rowfern on more than 4,000 models. Teams adopt it after a visible data
outage — a broken dashboard in a board meeting is a common origin story.

Pricing

The open-source engine is free. Cloud plans start at $500 per month per
workspace, scaling with models under contract.

About

Rowfern was founded in Denver in 2022 by two former data engineers. The
team is 19 people, remote across US time zones.

Security

Rowfern runs checks inside your warehouse or over a read-only connection; sampled data never leaves your environment unless you opt into cloud snapshots. SOC 2 Type II certified.
