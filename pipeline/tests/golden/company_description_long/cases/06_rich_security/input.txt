Vaultra — Every secret, every workload, accounted for

Vaultra discovers, rotates, and governs the non-human credentials that
modern infrastructure runs on: API keys, service-account tokens, database
passwords, signing certificates.

The problem

Machine identities now outnumber human ones by orders of magnitude, and
they leak: keys hard-coded in repositories, tokens shared across services,
credentials that outlive the engineer who created them. Breach reports
keep tracing incidents back to a forgotten credential.

How Vaultra works

Agentless discovery connects to AWS, GCP, Azure, Kubernetes, GitHub, and
your CI system, then builds an inventory of every credential, where it is
used, and what it can access. A risk engine ranks findings: unrotated keys,
over-privileged tokens, secrets in code. One-click rotation swaps a
credential and updates every consumer through native integrations, with a
rollback window. Policies enforce expiry and least privilege continuously,
not as a quarterly audit.

Compliance

Vaultra maps findings to SOC 2 and ISO 27001 controls and exports
audit-ready evidence. The platform itself is SOC 2 Type II certified.

Customers

Security teams at cloud-native companies from 50 to 5,000 employees.
The site cites deployments inventorying over 100,000 machine credentials.

Pricing is annual, tiered by number of identities under management.

About

Vaultra was founded in Austin, TX in 2022 by two security engineers who ran
