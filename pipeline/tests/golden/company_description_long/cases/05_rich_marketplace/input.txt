Forkfleet — The marketplace for used restaurant equipment

Forkfleet is where restaurants buy and sell commercial kitchen equipment:
ovens, ranges, refrigeration, prep tables, espresso machines. Every listing
is inspected, priced against market data, and shipped door to door.

Why Forkfleet exists

When a restaurant closes or renovates, its equipment traditionally moves
through auctioneers and local dealers at steep discounts — or sits in
storage. Buyers, meanwhile, face a used market with no condition standards
and no delivery. Forkfleet puts both sides on one platform.

The used-equipment market has always been big and broken. A six-burner
range that listed for $4,000 new is worth real money — but only if a buyer
can trust the burners fire, the buyer can get it to their kitchen, and the
seller does not have to spend three weekends fielding lowball calls.
Auctions solve none of that; classifieds solve less.

How it works

Sellers photograph equipment in the Forkfleet app and get a price estimate
from comparable sales. A Forkfleet inspector verifies condition — burners,
compressors, door seals, temperature calibration — before the listing goes
live with a graded condition report. Buyers see the report, financing
options, and a delivery quote. Forkfleet coordinates freight through
partner carriers, including liftgate delivery and old-equipment haul-away.

Selling on Forkfleet

Photograph the piece in the app, confirm the model number, and Forkfleet's
pricing engine returns an estimate from comparable completed sales in your
market — the same data dealers use, shown to you. Accept, and an inspector
visits within days to run the condition checks and shoot the standardized
photo set. Your listing goes live with its graded report; you approve
offers from the app; Forkfleet handles payment, freight booking, and the
paperwork. Payout lands after the buyer's acceptance window. Restaurants
mid-closure can list an entire kitchen in one inspector visit.

Buying on Forkfleet

Every listing carries a graded condition report — not seller photos and a
prayer. Grade A means inspected with no functional issues; Grade B means
cosmetic wear, fully functional; Grade C means a disclosed functional
issue, priced accordingly, with the defect documented. The listing shows
the inspector's checks: burner ignition, compressor amperage, door-seal
integrity, temperature calibration against a reference probe. Alongside
the report: a firm delivery quote to your address, financing terms from
our lending partner, and the 30-day working guarantee.

The inspection standard

Inspectors work a written protocol per category: ranges get every burner
fired and the oven cycled to temperature; refrigeration gets compressor
draw measured and pull-down time logged; espresso machines get pump
pressure and boiler checks. The protocol is public — buyers can read
exactly what "inspected" means — and inspectors photograph each checkpoint.
Equipment that fails inspection is either re-priced as Grade C with the
issue disclosed or declined.

Trust and economics

Every purchase carries a 30-day working guarantee. Forkfleet charges
sellers a commission of 8 percent per sale and buyers a flat service fee;
financing is offered through a lending partner. Nothing is consigned to a
warehouse — equipment ships directly from seller to buyer.

If a piece fails within 30 days of delivery, Forkfleet arranges repair,
replacement, or refund — the guarantee is Forkfleet's, not the seller's,
which is what the inspection layer pays for. The flat buyer fee is shown
before checkout; there are no listing fees, no monthly fees, and no
success theater. Sellers net the sale price minus the 8 percent commission,
period.

Delivery and logistics

Commercial kitchen equipment is heavy, awkward, and expensive to move
badly. Forkfleet books freight through vetted partner carriers with
restaurant-equipment experience: blanket wrap, liftgate delivery for
kitchens without a dock, inside placement to the kitchen line where
building access allows, and haul-away of the old equipment on the same
trip. Delivery quotes are firm at listing time — the price you see is the
price you pay — and damage in transit falls under the guarantee, not
under an argument with a carrier.

Markets

Since launching in Chicago in 2019, Forkfleet has expanded to 12 metro
markets and lists over 5,000 items at any time. Current markets: Chicago,
Milwaukee, Minneapolis, Detroit, Indianapolis, Columbus, Cleveland,
St. Louis, Kansas City, Nashville, Atlanta, and Dallas. Buyers can
purchase across markets; freight quotes price the distance automatically.
New markets open when inspector coverage is ready — the inspection
standard does not travel by wishful thinking.

What sells

Cooking: ranges, convection ovens, combi ovens, charbroilers, fryers,
griddles. Refrigeration: reach-ins, walk-in components, prep rails,
undercounters. Prep and storage: worktables, mixers, slicers, shelving.
Beverage: espresso machines, grinders, ice machines. The pricing engine
tracks comparable sales per category and market, so estimates reflect
what equipment actually clears at, not what a dealer hopes.

Frequently asked

Who owns the equipment while it is listed? The seller. Nothing is
consigned to a warehouse — equipment stays in place and ships directly
from seller to buyer once sold.

What if my equipment fails inspection? You can list at Grade C with the
issue disclosed and priced in, or decline to list. No fee either way.

Is financing available? Yes, through our lending partner, with terms
shown on every listing. Approval decisions typically come the same day.

Do you serve food trucks and ghost kitchens? Yes — anyone outfitting a
commercial kitchen buys the same inspected equipment.

About

Forkfleet was founded in Chicago in 2019 by Nate Kowalski and Maria Reyes.
Nate spent a decade as a restaurant-equipment dealer and auctioneer and
knew exactly where the margins hid; Maria built logistics software for a
freight brokerage. The founding insight: the used-equipment market did not
need another listing site — it needed condition standards and freight,
the two things neither side could supply alone.

The company is 45 people, headquartered in Chicago, IL: inspectors and
market managers in each metro, logistics coordinators, and the engineering
team behind the pricing engine and the app.

How the price estimate works

The pricing engine is built on completed Forkfleet sales: model, age,
grade, market, and season. A Grade B convection oven in Chicago prices
against what Grade B convection ovens actually sold for in Chicago in
the last two quarters — not against a dealer's asking price or a
depreciation curve from an accounting textbook. Sellers see the estimate
range and the count of comparable sales behind it; the listing price is
the seller's choice, but the estimate is where offers converge. When a
category runs hot — espresso equipment every spring — the estimates move
with it.

Selling a whole kitchen

Closures and renovations are Forkfleet's origin story and still the
busiest use case. One inspector visit can catalog an entire kitchen —
the range line, the walk-in, the prep tables, the smallwares lots — and
the seller approves the batch from the app. Landlords recovering
abandoned equipment and restaurant groups consolidating locations use
the same flow with volume support from a market manager. Equipment sells
from the space before the lease ends, which beats paying movers and
storage to defer the same decision.

Buying with confidence: the acceptance window

Delivery includes a 48-hour acceptance window: plug it in, run it, check
it against the condition report. A mismatch between the report and the
machine is Forkfleet's problem — refund or repair, buyer's choice. After
acceptance, the 30-day working guarantee covers functional failure. The
guarantee claims rate is published on the trust page, by category and
grade — Grade B refrigeration, our highest-volume category, runs under
3 percent.

For equipment dealers

Licensed dealers list on Forkfleet under the same inspection standard —
the graded report applies to everyone. Dealers get bulk listing tools
and a storefront page; buyers get the same guarantee whether the seller
is a closing bistro or a dealer with a warehouse. About a fifth of
listings come from dealers, and the standard is the point: buyers should
not have to care who the seller is.

More from the FAQ

Can I sell equipment that is still in use? Yes — many renovations list
weeks ahead with a pickup date; buyers see the availability window on
the listing.

Do you buy equipment outright? No. Forkfleet is a marketplace, not a
dealer: we never own inventory, which is why the condition report can
be honest.

What areas do you deliver to? Anywhere in the continental US — the 12
inspection markets are where sellers can list, but buyers purchase
from anywhere, with freight quoted to the door.

Is there a mobile app for buyers? Browsing and offers run on web and
mobile; the app's inspection tooling is what sellers and inspectors
use.
